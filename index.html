What is a Website Audit?

A website audit measures how easy it is to find, trust, and use your business online — then turns those insights into a clear plan for improvements.

Visibility & Accuracy

  • • Gap identification — find missing or outdated info (e.g., contact details)
  • • Findability — measure how quickly customers locate you via search and social
  • • Search results — ensure you appear prominently with accurate hours/address
  • • Digital channel presence — check visibility on website, social, and Google Maps

Experience & Consistency

  • • Conversion points — remove hurdles in ordering or contacting
  • • Customer engagement points — make it simple to reach or order anywhere
  • • Consistency check — align logo, colours, fonts, and tone across channels
  • • Mobile optimisation — ensure a seamless phone and tablet experience

Measurement & Growth

  • • Data performance — verify Google Analytics and platform insights are set up
  • • Track visibility, engagement, and traffic to inform decisions
  • • Turn findings into a prioritized action plan

Step-by-step guide to an audit

1) Define objectives Specify goals like identifying content gaps, improving search visibility, or boosting customer engagement.
2) Collect key data Use search results, social profiles, competitor pages, Google Analytics, and SEO tools to assess visibility and engagement.
3) Create an action plan Prioritize fixes like updating info, improving SEO, and streamlining contact or ordering across channels.
